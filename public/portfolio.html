About Me

Computers and technology have always been a huge part of my background. I grew up spending a majority of my days in front of a desktop computer or laptop, whether it was playing games, watching TV shows, or doing homework assignments. I mainly used my computer time for entertainment, but recently I’ve decided to utilize my above-average computer literacy to follow a more professional career path.

I had first learned about the basic web languages such as HTML, CSS, and JavaScript in a web development class I took in my junior year of high school. I wasn’t looking to become a web developer at the time, but I did do very well in that class. A year later, I graduated high school with the goal of going into animation and becoming a storyboard artist. Unfortunately, due to the poor state of the Western animation industry, and because I had no real direction after dropping out of community college, I eventually gave up on that career choice. I then realized that computers were my second calling, so I decided to take the path of a developer.

I started learning HTML and CSS again on my own, this time with both being more familiar to me in part to that class I took in high school. After that, I spent the next six months learning and studying JavaScript, doing every exercise I could to improve on it. I built a few projects on the side to further practice and apply everything I've learned, while learning new concepts in the process. A year later, I decided to return to college to get a degree in software engineering because I felt that a degree could open more opportunities for me. I’m currently looking for my very first position as a software engineer. and I’m continuing to learn and build new skills as consistently as possible to make that happen!
