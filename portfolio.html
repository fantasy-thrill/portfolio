About Me

Computers and technology have always been a huge part of my background. I grew up spending a majority of my days in front of a desktop computer or laptop, whether it was playing games, watching TV shows, or doing homework assignments. I mainly used my computer time for entertainment, but recently I’ve decided to utilize my above-average computer literacy to follow a more professional career path.

I had first learned about the basic web languages such as HTML, CSS, and JavaScript in a web development class I took in my junior year of high school. I wasn’t looking to become a web developer at the time, but I did do very well in that class. A year later, I graduated high school with the goal of going into animation and becoming a storyboard artist. Unfortunately, due to the poor state of the Western animation industry, that didn’t work out for me. I then realized that computers were my second calling, so I decided to take the path of a developer.

I started learning HTML and CSS again, this time with both being more familiar to me in part to that class I took in high school. Then I spent the next six months learning and studying JavaScript, doing every exercise I could to improve on it. I’m currently looking for my very first position as a front end web developer and I’m continuing to learn and build new skills as consistently as possible to make that happen!
